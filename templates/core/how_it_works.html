Why this exists

We built Steadfast Scholars around a few beliefs — the “five whys” behind everything you'll see:

  1. Why mastery, not grades? A percentage hides what a child actually understands. We track Beginning → Developing → Proficient → Mastered per piece of work, so you always know where they truly are — and they advance when they're ready, not when a calendar says so.
  2. Why a level per subject? A child can be years ahead in reading and right on pace in math. Each subject moves at its own level, so they accelerate where they shine and take their time where they need it.
  3. Why a separate, login-free kid portal? Children should see only their own work — one clear next step, no navigation, no wall of rows — so they can start on their own and feel capable, not overwhelmed.
  4. Why does the AI never grade alone? The assistant reads your child's work and proposes a mastery level with warm, specific feedback — but you always review and finalize. The teacher stays the teacher.
  5. Why the durable record? Homeschooling under a charter or state program means showing your work. Every day your child does builds a work log, mastery history, and a printable report your Educational Specialist can read at a glance.

What's inside

👧
Children & levels

Add each child with a school Level. Every subject then runs at its own grade.

📚
Curricula

Built-in courses (literature, writing, math) with lessons — plus online subjects like Beast Academy or DIVE that launch out to the provider, and resource links (answer keys, read-alouds) you pin per book.

🌱
The kid portal

A login-free page with just their subjects: “what's next,” tap a subject, do the work. Typing, matching, fill-in-the-blank, drawing on a sentence — with spelling help built in.

✅
Grading & the writing coach

When a child turns in work, the assistant gives them warm feedback (never a grade) and drafts a mastery level for you to confirm. On rough drafts, a writing coach offers gentle suggestions — it never rewrites their work.

📝
Work log & Progress

Every submission lands in the work log. The Progress page shows each subject's standing and mastery-over-time trends.

📄
Charter report

One click prints a date-range report — work log + mastery + trends — ready to save as a PDF for your reviewing teacher. See a sample →

How to use it — parents & teachers

  1. Add your children. Go to My Children → Add and set each child's Level.
  2. Set up subjects. Open Curricula. Use a built-in course, or add your own — tick “done on an external website” for online subjects. Then place each child at where they're starting.
  3. Hand over the device. On a child's page, tap Open portal (or copy the link and bookmark it on their tablet). They see only their work.
  4. They do the work. As they type it autosaves; when they turn it in, they get instant encouragement and you get a “Feedback to review” card on your home page.
  5. You confirm mastery. Open the draft, read what your child was told, accept or adjust the level, and finalize. That's what builds the record.
  6. Report anytime. From Progress, open Charter report, pick a date range, and print or save as PDF.

How it looks for your child

Their portal opens to “Hi! Here's what's waiting for you today.” They tap a subject, see the one next thing to do, and go. Finished work folds away; a subject they do online opens the website in a tap. When they turn something in, they get a kind note about their work — never a score. A small “Parents” button in the corner is the only way back to your side.

Sharing with a co-parent or teacher

From the account menu, choose Invite people to bring in a co-parent, guardian, grandparent, or reviewing teacher — with exactly the access you choose. Co-parents and guardians can add and edit; grandparents and teachers get read-only oversight, including the charter report.
